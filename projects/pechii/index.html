Pechii

A wardrobe archive and trading platform for the Lolita fashion community

Project Overview

Pechii is a mobile app concept designed to help members of the Lolita fashion community catalog, trade, and coordinate their wardrobe pieces. This project aims to solve the challenges of organizing extensive fashion collections, being able to try different pieces within budget, and staying connected with the Lolita community.

Problem Statement

Lolita fashion enthusiasts often struggle with:

  • Organizing and cataloging their extensive wardrobe collections
  • Finding trusted community members to trade or sell pieces
  • Keeping track of coordinate combinations and outfit ideas
  • Accessing a centralized platform dedicated to their niche fashion community
  • Being able to afford trying multiple pieces and styles at once
"How might we create a digital space that helps Lolita fashion enthusiasts organize their wardrobes and try different styles while connecting with their community?"

Research & Discovery

User Research

I researched online Lolita fashion community threads and Lolita fashion cost analysis papers to understand the community's pain points and needs:

Key Findings

  • Use spreadsheets to track items
  • Feel overwhelmed by wardrobe size
  • Want better trading options, apart from weekly/monthly swap meets
  • 100% active in online communities

User Needs

  • Visual wardrobe organization
  • Coordinate planning tools
  • Safe trading platform
  • Community features

Key Features

1. Digital Wardrobe Archive

Users can photograph and catalog every piece in their collection with detailed tags, purchase information, and condition notes.

2. Coordinate Builder

A drag-and-drop interface allows users to create and save outfit combinations from their archived pieces.

3. Community Marketplace

Safe, moderated trading platform with user verification and rating system.

4. Swipe or List View for Listings

Users can choose between swiping left/right or vertical-scrolling to view and add listings.

Challenges & Solutions

Challenge 1

Photo Quality Consistency

Users had varying photo quality for their items.

Solution: Implemented in-app photo guides and templates to ensure consistent, quality images.

Challenge 2

Trust & Safety

Users worried about scams in trading.

Solution: Created verification system, ratings, and secure payment integration.

Reflection & Learnings

This project strengthened my skills in emotional UI design, component systems, and designing for niche communities with strong identities. Key takeaways include:

  • Community-First Design: Understanding the unique culture and values of the Lolita fashion community was crucial to creating relevant features.
  • Visual Consistency: Balancing aesthetic beauty with functional clarity required careful iteration.
  • Trust Building: For marketplace features, establishing trust through design elements (verification badges, ratings) was essential.
  • Scalability: Planning for users with varying wardrobe sizes required flexible design solutions.
