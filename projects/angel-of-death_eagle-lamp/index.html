Angel of Death
Eagle Night Lamp

A 3D-printed, LED-lit wall lamp housed inside an iridescent eagle sculpture — its wing holes cast dramatic light patterns that transform any room into a different world depending on the color chosen.

About This Project

This piece started as an exploration of how light interacts with form. The eagle body is 3D-printed in iridescent filament that shifts between deep purple, electric blue, and teal depending on the angle. Inside its hollow cavity, an RGB LED strip throws light out through the wing-shaped cutouts, projecting dramatic feather-ray patterns across the surrounding wall and ceiling.

Hanging below the eagle's claws via braided cord is a secondary piece: a crescent moon with a seated figure, which glows on its own. The two together create a narrative scene — a guardian creature carrying the moon through the night.

The Complete Piece

Eagle and moon lamps together, wall-mounted in the dark. The wing-hole pattern radiates outward across the wall.

The Sculpture, Unlit

Without the light, you can appreciate the iridescent surface of the 3D-printed eagle. The filament is colored in a gradient between purple, cobalt, and teal depending on the viewing angle. The articulated claws grip the hanging cord, and the wing-slit geometry is visible along the side of the eagle's body.

The eagle lamp shown with a basket I personally crocheted, which holds a Shaymin (Pokemon) plushie. It is a commentary on prey vs. predator.

Light Color Modes

The RGB LED strip inside can be set to any color, or color-rotating mode. Each one brings on a different mood to the piece entirely. Cool blue reads like moonlight, red like embers, green like the woods.

Inside the Eagle

A reveal of the interior cavity, where the LED strip runs around the perimeter. A removable back panel gives access to the electronics and battery. The wing-slot geometry was engineered so that light shines through the holes in feather-like rays rather than a diffuse glow.

The Moon & Figure

The crescent moon and seated girl are printed separately and hang from the eagle's claws on braided cord. The moon is hollow with a small light inside, casting a warm glow around the figure perched on its edge. Together they read as an angel carrying the girl to the afterlife in the skies.

3D Modeling & Digital Fabrication

Before anything was printed, every part of the lamp was designed and rigged in Blender and Autodesk Fusion 360. The eagle body was split into 7 interlocking sections to fit within print bed limits, and custom boolean cutters were designed to create the wing-slot geometry that produces the light ray patterns. The moon girl figure was fully rigged with a skeleton to pose her sitting on the crescent before export.

Boolean Cutters

Custom cutter shapes were designed to boolean-subtract the wing slots and keyhole mount from the eagle body. Each shape was tuned to control the exact angle and width of the projected light rays.

The moon girl figure was rigged with a full skeleton in Blender, then posed into the seated position on the crescent before being exported for printing.
